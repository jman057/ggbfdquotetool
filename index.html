Funeral Directors Fee

All funeral directors charge a fixed service fee. It covers being available to make arrangements on your behalf, managing all required paperwork like the death and medical certificates, and a contribution towards their overheads

Committal of the body

The funeral director will quote for arranging the committal. This will include paperwork, transport and other costs specific to your selection.

Direct to committal

Most commonly, this option is for direct to cremation. Any service you arrange will not have the casket present, but might include the ashes.

Selecting this option will take you straight to the last page, as many services are no longer valid.

Viewing

It’s not for everyone, but for some it’s important to see their loved one, one last time. It’s the chance for a final kiss, or touch of the hand. Your funeral director will quote for preparation and presentation.

We explicitly would prefer not to embalm

Embalming adds financial and environmental costs to a funeral. Although it is simtimes necessary, especially for viewing, there are alternatsive options that can be disucssed with Selecting this option means that your quote will exclude options for embalming

Private Time / Visitation

An extended time at home, or an hour or two at the funeral home in the days before the service or committal, taking some private time is important for some families. This decision impacts transport arrangements and the funeral professional’s time.

Arranging a Service

There are any number of ways that families might arrange a Great Goodbye. Traditionally, the casket is present, and whether the service is formal or something less structured, this remains important for many. But families might choose to have the ashes instead of the casket, or opt for a memorial service at a later date.

Transport

Driving your loved one to the service and then on to the committal is a special way to get involved and personalise a Great Goodbye. For some people, taking this last drive together is deeply meaningful. If that’s not for you, your funeral professional will be honoured to do this task on your family’s behalf.

But there is no law that says you can’t transport the casket yourself, and you might wish to step beyond historic convention and do something a little different.

Of course, if you are having the service at home you will not need to specify funeral transport beyond having the casket delivered to the crematorium or burial ground.
